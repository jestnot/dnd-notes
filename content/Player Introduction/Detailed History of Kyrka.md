# Age of Stability/Stagnancy (the previous age)
For the past 600 years, the continent of Kyrka (pronounced “KEER-kah”) was governed by the royal Ekedalia family of elves in the the [[content/History of Kyrka/Ages/Age of Stagnancy]] (then referred to as the "Age of Stability"). Residing in [[content/World of Kyrka/Places/Ekedalen (no longer existant)/Ekedalian Places/Dalia]], the holy city of Ekedalen, the royal family held a firm grip over the main source of freshwater on the continent and employed a feudal system to manage their land and people:

- Monarch: The head of [[content/World of Kyrka/Places/Ekedalen (no longer existant)/Ekedalian Government/The Ekedalia Family]] and ruler of the Ekedalian kingdom
- Nobles: The monarch chose the head of each noble family, and these were only comprised of the longest-lifespan races (specifically, elves, gnomes, and dwarves)
	- Each noble family was appointed a demesne by the monarch, with the more favored families closer to Ekedalen
	- The monarch reserved the right to revoke power at any time, and was strict on keeping noble families pure to their race (i.e. having a half-elf child was fair ground for the entire family losing their nobility status)
	- The head of each family was an inherited position
- Knights & Druids: Each noble appointed a Knight (head of security) and Druid (head of agriculture) to protect and support their land
	- These positions were not necessarily inherited, although many were for generations
	- These positions did not need to strictly be given to elves/gnomes/dwarves, but they often were given to a person with a longer lifespan
- Serfs: The remainder (and majority) of the population

Throughout the [[content/History of Kyrka/Ages/Age of Stagnancy]], Kyrka experienced the longest period of peace in recent history, as well as the fewest changes in leadership. [[content/World of Kyrka/Places/Ekedalen (no longer existant)/Ekedalian Government/The Ekedalia Family]] heavily emphasized the value of experience and wealth of knowledge only longer-lifespan races could provide, especially in the arcane arts (which were exclusively taught to the royal family, nobility, knights, and druids). The lands closest to [[content/World of Kyrka/Places/Ekedalen (no longer existant)/Ekedalian Places/Dalia]] (and the source of freshwater) were the most prosperous, but no Kyrkan had to worry about their safety or hunger, thanks to the disciplined knights and druids.

As with ages past, the Ekedalia family led Kyrka in worshipping the Sisters who created mortals. When [[content/World of Kyrka/Places/Ekedalen (no longer existant)/Ekedalian Government/First Ekedalian King]] first took the throne, [[The Church]] claimed that the Ekedalian family was chosen directly by [[The Three Sisters]] to bless the land with their rule. 100 years into the Age of Stability, the Church received a prophecy from the Sisters themselves, warning that mortals would one day be tested to prove their worth. That prophecy came true 197 years ago, when Kyrka was ripped apart by the gargantuan monster now known as [[Domedag]] ("DOH-meh-DAHG"), and Kyrkans were forced to face the Sisters' judgment.

Domedag's arrival was foreshadowed by a series of natural disasters mentioned in the prophecy. As soon as the Church confirmed these events, [[content/World of Kyrka/Places/Ekedalen (no longer existant)/Ekedalian Government/First Ekedalian King]] began putting his plan six centuries in the making into motion. The Ekedalia family set up magical defenses around Ekedalen and stockpiled supplies, believing that the Sisters would deliver the holy city to safety. Many nobles fled to Ekedalen for shelter, leaving their knights, druids, and serfs behind. However, Domedag emerged from the ground in the center of Kyrka, destroying Ekedalen and its citizens rapidly.

Although powerful mages in Ekedalen were able to survive the initial attack of Domedag burrowing out of the ground, they quickly realized that Domedag produced a natural antimagic field, and fell prey to the beast; their attacks were said to have bounced off its carapace harmlessly.

As Domedag began its trail of destruction around the nearby demesnes, Kyrka descended into chaos. Without leadership, the remaining nobles fled to the coasts and argued on how to take down Domedag. One brave human serf named [[Nackan Qvist]] rallied the knights, druids, and serfs, uniting them under a common goal: to stop Domedag from destroying their home.

Under Nackan's leadership, this group used much riskier tactics than those employed by the Ekedalia family or nobility, culminating in a final attack where Nackan and a squadron of fierce warriors volunteered to be swallowed by Domedag to land the killing blow from within. This group notably did not wield magic, but were strong martial fighters, quick to adapt to new situations, and the bravest of all Kyrkans.

While the squadron was victorious in felling Domedag and saving the remains of Kyrka, they were never seen again and presumed to have died in the belly of the beast. Domedag's corpse lies at the heart of Kyrka today, still largely intact. In the chaos of the months following Domedag's demise, various unidentified scavengers attempted to scrape pieces of Domedag's carapace, but most were unsuccessful in making a dent. The hole it emerged from also remains—it is currently unconfirmed how deep it goes.

Following Domedag's death, Nackan's lieutenants gathered to discuss how to establish , determining:
- The establishment of [[content/World of Kyrka/Places/The Three Nations/Nation - Nackanas/Nackanas]] to the northwest
- The establishment of [[content/World of Kyrka/Places/The Three Nations/Nation - Pinntorp/Pinntorp]] to the northeast
- The establishment of [[content/World of Kyrka/Places/The Three Nations/Nation - Vangsta/Vangsta]] to the south, to be led by a collective of nobles who contributed to Nackan's cause by providing resources
- The land surrounding Domedag's corpse would be considered neutral territory and henceforth known as [[Domedag's Abyss]]
	- The three nations agreed to protect their third of the border and combine efforts to protect Kyrka from anything that might emerge from the hole
	- After realizing the value of the finite materials gathered from Domedag's corpse, the Three Nations agreed that no single nation was allowed to harvest from the corpse without the other two nations' consent
		- If the Three Nations agreed to gather pieces, they needed to put it into contract
			- e.g. the Three Nations could agree to all contribute equal resources to the harvesting and split any gained resources
			- e.g. one nation could offer other goods/services in exchange for being allowed to harvest a certain part of the corpse, with the other two nations sending representatives to confirm and oversee the harvesting

While this discussion was contentious (particularly about Vangsta—its monopoly over precious stones and metals gave them a strong advantage in land negotiations), the group left in agreement that this marked the beginning of a new age: the Age of Progress. The old age would henceforth be renamed the "Age of Stagnancy."

# Age of Progress (the current age)
Following the fall of [[Domedag]], the revolution continued and sparked the new [[content/History of Kyrka/Ages/Age of Progress]]. The world shifted from praising "slow but safe" ideas towards focusing on "high risk, high reward" tactics as the best means to gain progress.

During the first decade, the three nations focused their efforts on rebuilding and keeping the peace. Monsters that were once kept at bay during the [[content/History of Kyrka/Ages/Age of Stagnancy]] now roamed wild, taking advantage of the new governments' focus on law and rebuilding over border control. The coastal populations of [[content/World of Kyrka/Places/The Three Nations/Nation - Nackanas/Nackanas]] and [[content/World of Kyrka/Places/The Three Nations/Nation - Pinntorp/Pinntorp]] have seen the most loss in recent years, between the resurgence of monsters and various pirate attacks. (There is no coastal population in Vangsta, due to the position of the Vesken Mountains.)

Ever since Domedag's death, [[The Three Sisters]] have gone silent. In the Age of Stagnancy, the most pious everyday citizen used to be able to hear words of wisdom as an answer to their prayers, but in the Age of Progress, prayers seem to go unanswered.

# Belief in the Three Sisters
Pinntorp and Nackanas largely believe they have passed the Sisters’ test, and no longer need them to continue living life.
- This is for various reasons (e.g. the Sisters approve of Kyrka's new way of living, and are leaving Kyrka alone)
- In any case, the Church has very little presence in either nation, and all temples have been converted into other public spaces (e.g. schools, libraries, shelters, or museums to preserve the old temple architecture)

**Nackanians** believe that Kyrkans have passed the Sisters’ test by proving that mortals do not need magic to survive—after all, Domedag was not defeated by mages, but by brave warriors.
- Nackanians strongly believe that mortals were not intended to use magic by the Sisters, and the Ekedalia family's heavy use of it is proof that magic is a sin that the unworthy fall prey to. The Ekedalia family hoarded magic for themselves and were selfish rulers, which caused the Sisters to send Domedag to Kyrka in the first place.

**Pinntorpers** believe that Kyrkans have passed the Sisters’ test by proving that they are worthy of ruling themselves; the Ekedalia royal family and feudalism setup was considered wrong by the Sisters, and the new meritocracy structure is how the Sisters intended for mortals to live.
- Pinntorpers believe that it is not magic that is inherently evil, but the way that the Ekedalia family was using it; the Sisters intended for knowledge of magic to be shared to all who desired it.

**Vangstans** don't have any official stance on the Sisters; they are free to practice their religious beliefs as long as they pay their taxes. Naturally, the only official remaining presence of the Church and continued use of its temples are in Vangsta.

# The Current State of Kyrka

## Nackanas
Nackanas' government consists of the **Prime Minister** and their **cabinet ministers** (appointed by the Prime Minister and covering topics like justice, foreign affairs, health, and education). The Prime Minister is appointed by the Council, a group of elected representatives from across Nackanas. Each Nackanian citizen is granted a vote for their region's Council member when they are of age. Each government position has a fixed term of no more than a few years.

[[The Church]] has a new branch and strong presence in Nackanas (having largely faded from the public eye elsewhere in Kyrka): [[Extremist Church Branch]]. They urge Nackanians to live modestly as [[Nackan Qvist]] did to prove themselves to the Sisters—the only way mortals can spare Kyrka from a future test like Domedag is to reject opulence and focus on the gifts of the earth. The Church and the Nackanian government both reject any studies of the arcane arts, believing it to be the ultimate temptation that [[content/World of Kyrka/Places/Ekedalen (no longer existant)/Ekedalian Government/The Ekedalia Family]] fell prey to, which caused the Sisters to send Domedag to test mortals. After all, Nackan was able to rally Kyrkans and defeat Domedag without any arcane powers. The most devout Church members take on a new family name "Qvist," which is also given to any orphans taken in by the Church.

Immediately after its establishment, the Nackanian government put an emphasis on scientific research—an area of academia that was always forbidden during the Age of Stagnancy as inferior to the arcane arts and seen as a waste of time and resources.

## Pinntorp
Pinntorp has no central governing body; all decisions are made through citizen voting. [[content/World of Kyrka/Places/The Three Nations/Nation - Pinntorp/Pinn Government/The Regulation]] is a group of officials elected for their prowess in calculation—they are responsible for conducting national votes and calculating how many votes each Pinntorper citizen gets. Each Pinntorper's number of votes is updated annually based on a complicated (and public) calculation to determine how much that Pinntorper has contributed to society, with a heavy emphasis on academic contribution to the arcane arts.

[[The Church]] does not have a strong presence in Pinntorp—Pinntorpers largely believe that the arcane arts were not what angered the Sisters, but the Ekedalia family's belief that long-lifespan races were inherently worthy of more than others. Pinntorp was founded by those who believed that Kyrkans passed the Sisters’' test by banding together and proving their worth. Pinntorp has founded new schools and libraries for the arcane arts—now open to peoples of all lifespans, for the first time in centuries. With vital resources being allocated to rebuilding and defending the nation, Pinntorp diverts its academic funding towards the arcane arts, believing it to be more important to spread the knowledge to the people who were excluded for so long, rather than invest in the new area of scientific research.

In case of a world-threatening emergency (on the scale of Domedag), a group of the highest achieving scholars (i.e. the citizens with the most votes) called [[content/World of Kyrka/Places/The Three Nations/Nation - Pinntorp/Pinn Government/The Board]] is given temporary governing power. The Board consists of the highest achieving representative of the eight schools of magic, as well as a ninth member for tiebreaking—the ninth member is the most well-rounded scholar across all schools of magic, although they may not necessarily specialize in one.

## Vangsta
Vangsta is ruled by [[content/World of Kyrka/Places/The Three Nations/Nation - Vangsta/Vangstan Government/Gamvaluta]] (pronounced ("GAHM-vah-LOO-tah," meaning "Old Money" in dwarvish), a group of dwarves descended from noble families from the Age of Stagnancy. Gamvaluta resides in Ivar, the only city with access to the mines of the Vesken Mountains, and holds a monopoly over most precious stones and metals in Kyrka. These resources cannot be found elsewhere on the continent (there are a few small mines elsewhere, but only with common stones and metals) and are used in construction as well as both arcane and scientific research (i.e. to create necessary equipment).

[[content/World of Kyrka/Places/The Three Nations/Nation - Vangsta/Vangstan Places/Ivar]] is known as the most fortified city of Kyrka—the only people allowed into its gates are those who reside in Ivar. All trades are made in the heavily defended area immediately outside its entrance, known as "[[The Pass]]."

There are no coastal cities along the southern side of Vangsta, due to the [[Vesken Mountains]] spanning the coast and being adjacent to the [[Southern Sea]], which in turn is filled with craggy rocks and often stormy. There are no sea trade routes from Vangsta, but most land routes in Kyrka lead to [[content/World of Kyrka/Places/The Three Nations/Nation - Vangsta/Vangstan Places/Vinterfint]], a prominent city near [[Domedag's Abyss]]—at the intersection of the three nations.

While some Vangstans have formed [[content/World of Kyrka/Places/The Three Nations/Nation - Vangsta/Vangstan Government/Nyvaluta]] ("NAI-vah-LOO-tah," meaning "New Money" in dwarvish) in an attempt to oppose Gamvaluta's absolute and stubborn policies, Gamvaluta's strong economic power over vital resources for construction and both arcane and scientific research has prevented Nyvaluta from gaining traction. Any Vangstans who publicly speak against Gamvaluta are considered part of Nyvaluta. In order to enforce their rule, Gamvaluta has enforced heavy taxes on members of Nyvaluta—those who cannot pay are sent to the jail within Ivar.

## The Church
[[The Church]]'s remaining devout followers largely reside in Vangsta. Some of the Church members claim to read signs from [[The Three Sisters]] as answers to their prayers, but the average Kyrkan will tell you that the Church is just seeing what they want to see (i.e. the Sisters are silent and the Church is making it up out of desperation or craziness). However, no follower (even previous clerics and paladins) have claimed to directly communicate with the Sisters since Domedag first appeared.

Clerics and paladins who gained power from the Sisters in the Age of Stagnancy have formed [[Extremist Church Branch]] and gathered in [[content/World of Kyrka/Places/The Three Nations/Nation - Vangsta/Vangstan Places/Extremist Church Branch Village]], where they have a temple and farmland. This is open to anyone who seeks food and shelter, as long as they are willing to pay their respects to the Sisters and participate in regular religious rites.

**Pinntorpers** frown upon the use of "divine magic," believing that the so-called-pious are actually channeling arcane magic from the same source, and incorrectly attributing it to Sisters who are no longer listening (when it is actually coming from their practiced use of vocal, somatic, and material components). They care more about the misconstrued source than the use of magic itself.

**Nackanians** frown upon the use of divine magic in general, believing that all magic should not be used by mortals.

**Vangstans** have a mixed reaction to the use of divine magic; most powerful Vangstans closer to Ivar care more about money than arguing over religion, but the common people throughout the rest of the nation have a wide range of beliefs. Some are receptive to divine magic if it means getting help with ailments quickly, and some still harbor distrust or pity in the Church.
