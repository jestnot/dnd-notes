# Long Ago
Eons ago during the [[Age of Origins]], [[The Three Sisters]] created the world, including the continent of Kyrka and its inhabitants. As the almighty gods, they guided their creations through the ages, sometimes speaking directly to the pious and providing cryptic prophesies. While there are many legends of their feats, none can be confirmed today, as they have gone silent after their last prophesized test -- meant to judge if mortals could prove their worth.

This test is widely accepted to have been completed by the death of [[Domedag]], a massive creature who burrowed out of the earth and swallowed [[Dalia]] (the capital city of the Kingdom of [[Ekedalen]]) at the end of the last age (the [[Age of Stagnancy (or Stability)]]). Domedag’s arrival plunged Kyrka into chaos, as the Kingdom had kept undisputed power and arcane knowledge in the hands of the few in power. Most such long-lifespan peoples (namely elves, dwarves, and gnomes) were killed in the destruction Domedag left in its wake, but thankfully, [[Nackan Qvist]] (a human serf) rallied the remaining Kyrkans and led the charge to kill Domedag. Domedag notably had natural antimagic features and Nackan did not possess any arcane abilities, but she nonetheless delivered the killing blow from the inside, willingly sacrificing herself to the beast.

Older legends tell of how [[The Three Sisters]] shared prophecies and powers with creatures of the past, and even created artifacts -- although many of these are considered myths. One such legend tells of [[The Siphon]], which is said to have the power to both siphon all magical ability from a creature, and grant it to another. No such artifacts have been publicly recorded in recent ages, although some legends tell of their use in eons past.
# Present Day
**197 years** into the [[Age of Progress]], Kyrkans have begun to rebuild and experiment with new governments and academic pursuits. Ambition is widely seen as the most valuable attribute, with many using the phrase "high risk, high reward" to justify their decisions.
## The Three Nations
In the northwestern Republic of [[Nackanas]], Nackanians (also called “Nacks”) largely believe that [[Nackan Qvist]]’s victory over [[Domedag]] passed [[The Three Sisters]]’ test and proved that mortals do not need magic (as [[The Ekedalia Family]] relied on) to live as the Sisters intended. Many new areas of scientific research have been kickstarted, spurring a wave of frequent discoveries. However, its new government struggles to defend its borders against monster attacks. The Kingdom of [[Ekedalen]] had a much stronger hand and martial prowess, whereas many current-day Nackanians are now pursuing science and education (creating a larger middle class).

In the northeastern Meritocracy of [[Pinntorp]], Pinntorpers (also called “Pinns”) largely believe that Kyrkans passed the Sisters’ test by dismantling the Kingdom and sharing education and rights amongst all humanoid races. Public schools and libraries have been built, and shorter-lifespan races have been welcomed into the top Kyrkan universities for the first time. However, mages find themselves negotiating for precious Vangstan resources for the first time, as Nackanas competes for the same materials in their scientific research. Pinntorp has strong magical defenses against monster attacks, but has focused more on rebuilding and developing a few areas (rather than across their land), compared to the other two nations.

In the southern Plutocracy of [[Vangsta]], there is no official stance on the Sisters’ test; what matters is economic power. [[Gamvaluta]] (the governing group) maintains a monopoly over precious stones and metals, which are now traded to both Pinntorp (to be used as arcane material components and for arcane technology) and Nackanas (to be used in new scientific research). [[Nyvaluta]] (a relatively new grassroots movement) is garnering more respect from the Pinntorpers and Nackanians due to their openness towards foreigners, to Gamvaluta's chagrin. However, Gamvaluta maintains its strong hold over power in Vangsta due to their total control over the mines in the [[Vesken Mountains]] -- where they have maintained their centuries-old policy of banning foreigners from entering.
## Religion
Because [[The Three Sisters]] have been silent since Domedag's defeat, most temples have been converted to other public spaces. Nowadays, few people practice religious rituals in public, except the new Nackanian branch in Nackanas. Generally, Kyrkans believe they have passed the Sisters’ test (although they disagree on why) and have resolved to live life in the manner that garnered the Sisters’ approval.

Today, the largest remaining branch of [[The Church]] resides in [[Extremist Church Branch Village]], a Vangstan village. Most Kyrkans regard them as delusional, as they believe [[The Three Sisters]]’ last prophecy hasn’t yet been fulfilled -- and it only will be when [[The Siphon]] is destroyed, as proof that mortals reject the power that belongs to Sisters. [[Nackanas]] and [[Pinntorp]] believe this group is potentially dangerous and keep a close eye on any members within their borders, but the [[Vangsta]] does not have any public qualms with their existence, so long as they maintain their farmlands and continue to pay taxes.
## Magic and Science
Recent scientific discoveries have caused tension between [[Nackanas]] and [[Pinntorp]], as they both desire the same resources that can only be found in the [[Vesken Mountains]]. [[Vangsta]] has emerged from this conflict as the undisputed economic power on Kyrka (to [[Gamvaluta]]'s delight). Trade disputes have been resolved with coin and tense diplomatic actions (including stricter border patrols) thus far.
