This will be a long-form campaign set in the homebrewed world of [[Storvald]], on the continent of [[Kyrka]]: a land once united as the Kingdom of [[Ekedalen]]. After a gargantuan creature erupted from the earth and destroyed the capital, Kyrkans banded together to defeat it, and then split into [[The Three Nations]].

With the dissolution of the monarchy, two nations disagree over whether the arcane arts should be taught to all or replaced with a new area of study: the sciences. While the third nation has remained neutral, rumors of extremist groups are whispered taverns across the continent -- especially after the end of the recent [[Nackanas-Pinntorp War]].
# Intro to Kyrka
[[Kyrka]] ("KEER-kah") is currently marked by the borders of four notable areas: the Three Nations ([[Nackanas]], [[Pinntorp]], and [[Vangsta]]) and [[Domedag's Abyss]].

Several centuries ago, [[The Three Sisters]] (the god creators of this world, Kyrka, and its inhabitants) prophesized the coming of a gargantuan creature as a test of judgement for humanoids. **197 years** ago, a creature (later referred to as "[[Domedag]]") erupted from the center of Kyrka, destroying the capital city of the Kingdom of [[Ekedalen]]. As the majority of arcane power had been hoarded by the monarchy, [[Nackan Qvist]] (a human serf) rose to the challenge of rallying the surviving Kyrkans to band together and defeat Domedag -- but sacrificed herself in the process.

In the aftermath of Domedag's defeat, two main ideologies on magic emerged. These ideologies are not strictly followed across borders (or in general); they're just the two main mindsets that pushed Kyrkans to split into [[The Three Nations]]. However, some people tend toward the extremes of the spectrum, and even have ambitions to make their school of though law. 
- **"Arcane knowledge belongs to all. The kingdom fell and Domedag was able to cause widespread destruction because the monarchy was greedy with their power."**
	- "[[The Ekedalia Family]] was wrong to control knowledge and only teach the arcane arts to the upper class (the minority of Kyrkans, residing in the capital and ruling as nobles throughout the rest of Kyrka)."
	- This is the prevailing ideology in [[Pinntorp]], in the northeast.
- **"Arcane knowledge is dangerous; humanoids are not meant to wield such power. Domedag was sent to Kyrka to remove those who arrogantly wielded it.**
	- "Nackan Qvist and her followers defeated Domedag without being powerful mages. Humanoids were never meant to wield such power, and [[The Ekedalia Family]]'s hubris caused their downfall."
	- This is the prevailing ideology in [[Nackanas]] (named after [[Nackan Qvist]]), in the northwest. Nackanians have focused their education and research around the relatively new area of "the sciences" since Domedag's demise.

## The Legend of the Siphon
[[The Siphon]] is an object of legend, said to have the power to both siphon all magical ability from a creature, and grant it to another. No such artifacts have been publicly recorded in recent ages, although some legends tell of their use in eons past. Many assume [[The Three Sisters]] had something to do with its creation, but no one seems to know for sure; there have been many retellings of this legend over the ages and no official record.
### Note for Players
While most Kyrkans have heard at least one legend that referenced the Siphon (as most people would have heard of the Holy Grail, Excalibur, or the Ark of the Covenant in the western part of our world), there haven't been any new public discoveries and it isn't a common topic of conversation with most Kyrkans.

# Campaign Tone
- **Heroic** - This campaign is intended to frame the party as heroes! Unlike Jessie's Eberron campaign, the major factions the party will interact with are intended to have a more black-and-white stance on morality.
- **Noblebright Fantasy** -"Noblebright fantasy is not utopian fiction. The world of a noblebright story is not perfect, and indeed can sometimes be quite dark. Actions have consequences, and even good characters can make terrible mistakes. But a noblebright story is generally hopeful in tone, even if there are plenty of bad, grim, dark things going on in the world.” (CJ Brightley)
- **Mid-level of Magic** - Magic is prevalent in the world, from the arcane infrastructure of Pinntorp to the divine power that the Three Sisters granted to followers of [[dnd-notes/content/World of Kyrka/People and Organizations/The Church/The Church|The Church]].
# Campaign Theme: "Is it worth it to break the cycle?"
You can also think of this theme as “traditionalist vs. modern values.”
- When does it make sense to carry on tradition vs. upend the status quo?
- How do we make that decision -- and who should decide?

This is the main question we'll explore in the main story -- and ideally in any side and PC-specific stories! Consider it when thinking about your PC's backstory and the arc(s) you want them to go through. The stories in this campaign are intended to have characters with different answers to this question, and different situations where the question is applicable.
## Theme Examples
Examples of what "the cycle" can refer to:
- A person who gets locked into a certain way of thinking, but stubbornly refuses to change their mind
- A parent-child relationship within a family over the course of generations (e.g. a cycle of neglect)
- A court ruling that has become the standard for any new and similar cases
- A government system that works well for most people, but poorly for a few
	- Maybe it would require lots of time, money, and effort to go through all the red tape required to push a new policy
- A society-wide tradition that is done because "that's how things have always been done"
# Homebrewed Rules
## Healing is Necromantic Magic
Healing magic is considered under the school of necromancy, including the following spells:
- Level 1: Cure Wounds, Healing Word
- Level 2: Prayer of Healing
- Level 3: Beacon of Hope, Mass Healing Word
- Level 5: Mass Cure Wounds
- Level 7: Heal
- Level 9: Mass Heal, Power Word Heal

This is done for narrative purposes, so we may need to make changes to specific spells and magical effects if this causes any balance issues.
