**Before** you begin creating your character, read the [[Campaign Overview]]!

You do **NOT** need to read the [[Abridged History of Kyrka]] or the [[Detailed History of Kyrka]], but you can if you'd like. 🙂
# PC Requirements
- You are an adult (i.e. no underage characters)
- You are not evil
- You have a strong understanding of and tie to at least one of the Three Nations (e.g. because you were born and raised there)
- You are aware of [[The Siphon]], as most Kyrkans are (but you do not *have to* have a special tie to / interest in it... although it is encouraged)
# Party Requirements
- The party needs to have at least 2 different PC origin nations (of [[Nackanas]], [[Pinntorp]], and [[Vangsta]]), i.e. so the party has a variety of world views
	- You can totally have a PC who has spent time in multiple nations if you want; this is just to avoid having all PCs having spent their entire lives in a single nation
- The party has at least one PC of a race with a longer lifespan: an elf, gnome, dwarf, halfling, or some other race you get DM approval on

Once we have at least basic ideas of all the PCs, the DM will start working with the players to figure out which PCs have met prior to Session 1.
## Interwoven Backstories
The DM will try to interweave PC backstory elements (e.g. NPCs or events) with the main campaign and/or other PCs' backstories where possible!

As part of character creation, let the DM know if there are specific elements of your PC's backstory that:
- You do NOT want woven into the main campaign and/or other PCs' backstories
	- e.g. If there is an NPC in your PC's backstory that you do not want to make an appearance in the campaign
- Your PC *believes* is true, but may not be
	- i.e. You are okay leaving it open to the DM's interpretation -- and potentially surprising you during the campaign!
	- e.g. If your PC believes that an NPC died because they saw the NPC fall off a cliff, the DM *could* later say, "actually, the NPC survived the fall!" and have this NPC make an appearance in the campaign.
- You want to be objectively true (opposite of above)
	- e.g. If your PC believes that an NPC died, and you want that to be true. You don't want the DM to somehow plan a surprise resurrection as part of a potential story beat in the campaign.
# Character Sheet Notes
- Starting level = 5
	- Use point buy for starting stats
	- For leveling up HP, roll twice and take the higher number
	- You can start with 1 Common/Uncommon/Rare item and 1 Common/Uncommon item
- You may use official content, but DM gets final say in what makes sense within the world and campaign
	- If you are using any content that isn't mentioned in the setting information (e.g. a race that isn't mentioned), consider how it would work in the world/campaign and bring a proposal to the DM
	- If you want to use any homebrewed work, you need DM approval
## Choosing A Race
The fact that races have different average lifespans is essential to understanding Storvald's history. Specifically, longer-lifespan races that have a natural aptitude for the arcane (most notably elves and gnomes, but also dwarves) were able to seize and maintain power during the [[Age of Stagnancy]]. In the current [[Age of Progress]], shorter-lifespan races have gained more rights and power, leading to a more equitable society.

Here are the average lifespans for common races in Kyrka. If you are interested in playing a race not on this list (or an Underdark subrace), please talk to the DM!

# Character Backstory Questions
## Required
1. Which of the Three Nations are you from?
	1. If you spent significant time in multiple nations, which? Why?
2. Were you alive in the Age of Stagnancy?
	1. If yes, how were you affected by Domedag? How were your friends and family affected?
3. What is your stance on the arcane arts and the sciences?
4. What does your believe about the Three Sisters (the gods)?
	1. Specifically, what do you believe about their prophecy with Domedag?
	2. Why do you think they are silent? (Or... are they not to you?)
5. What do you know about the legend of [[The Siphon]]?
	- Are you well-informed of it and actively seeking it out? (e.g. to destroy an enemy’s power, to remove a powerful curse, to gain your own power, to sell for money or to repay a debt)
	- Or do you just know as much about it as the average Kyrkan?
6. What does your PC look like? (race, height, weight, physicality, outfit at the start of the campaign, fashion sense, and any other defining physical characteristics you want to share)
7. Who are (at least) 2 people/creatures/beings that are extremely important to your PC? What is your PC's relationship with them? Where are they now?
## Optional
1. How do you expect or want your PC to change over the course of the campaign?
	1. Is there a specific way you want your PC to change or grow? Is there a lesson you want them to learn?
2. For the nation(s) your PC has spent the most time in, how does your PC feel about their government?
	1. How does your PC feel about other nations' governing styles?
3. How does your PC's race's average lifespan affect your character and their relationships?
	1. Do you have a longer lifespan than a loved one? Have you known someone all your life who never seems to age?
