The Siphon is an ancient magical artifact of legend, supposedly able to siphon magical energy from one creature and transfer it to another.

An average Kyrkan would know:
- There have been many retellings/reinterpretations of the Siphon's legend over the years, but these are the most consistent parts
	- It has the ability to siphon magical energy from one creature and transfer it to another
	- It is a VERY powerful magical object
	- It was created sometime before the [[content/History of Kyrka/Ages/Age of Chaos]], and is one of the oldest artifacts of legend
		- Many believe [[The Three Sisters]] had something to do with its creation, but this is unclear/inconsistent
		- Because the Siphon is so old
	- Its current whereabouts are unknown to the public; there hasn't been a corroborated documented use of it in thousands of years
		- There are various stories/rumors/theories about if the Siphon still exists (or ever did), and where it is today
- Most Kyrkans know about the Siphon (as they do about other various legends in Kyrka), but it doesn't something the average Kyrkan thinks about often (see "Notes for Players" below)

# Notes for Players
> *How well-known or commonly thought about is the Siphon to the average Kyrkan?*
> 	A good analogy is the Holy Grail in *Indiana Jones and the Last Crusade*. Most people know that the Holy Grail is an object described in Arthurian legend, but they don't think about it often. Many may believe that the Holy Grail does/did not actually exist (or at least with the full magical power described in the legend), or has now been lost to time. However, there are some (like the characters in the movie) who believe it's real and has true power -- and are actively searching for it.
> 	The average person has heard about the Siphon (and other magical artifacts of legend) in the same way that the average person in the U.S. has heard of the Holy Grail, Excalibur, the Golden Fleece, the Ark of the Covenant, Mjolnir, Santa's sleigh, Baba Yaga's house, or the Philosopher's Stone. People have likely heard stories about these objects and know the gist of what they can supposedly do. People do not necessarily believe that the item is real or that it truly did what the legend described (e.g. perhaps because the stories have been embellished over the years).
