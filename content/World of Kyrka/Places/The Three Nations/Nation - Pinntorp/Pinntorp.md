Citizens are referred to as "Pinntorpers" or "Pinns."
# Government
The governing bodies are [[The Regulation]] and [[The Board]].

- Each Pinntorper is granted a number of votes based a complicated calculation of their contribution to society, with a heavy focus on their contribution to their academic contribution to the arcane arts.
- [[The Regulation]] is in charge of recalculating each citizen's votes annually.
- [[The Board]] is the presiding group of high-ranking scholars who make governing decisions.
	- The Board consists of **nine** members: the most prominent scholar in each of the 8 schools of magic and the most well-rounded Pinntorper scholar across all schools of magic.
## Voting System
- Each citizen's votes are based on how many degrees they have and how many references their publications receive.
- Each vote is linked to a school of magic -- the one that the citizen received a degree in or wrote a publication on.
	- Each degree and project must be assigned to ONE school of magic; some projects are related to multiple schools of magic, but are officially classified as only one -- the one that will give the researchers more sway with votes.
	- Over time, this has encouraged people in power to specialize in one school of magic per family.
- When citizens cast votes, they go to the relevant Board member for that school of magic.
	- The Board members can deliberate decisions, but the number of votes is used as a tie-breaker if the 8 members cannot come to a consensus.
		- If this happens and the skew of votes does not have a clear majority (i.e. higher than 66%), the 9th Board member has absolute power to make the final call.
# Known For
- Arcane studies (including arcane technology).
	- New schools and libraries which are open to all citizens.
	- The greatest advancements in the arcane arts to date.
	- The average Pinntorper knows at least a cantrip and even the smallest towns to use common magic items to improve their everyday life.
